1 · The art of constraints

In most digital art a pixel is meaningless. In pixel art the opposite is true: every pixel is a decision you make on purpose. You work small, with a limited palette, and let the viewer's eye assemble the rest. That limitation isn't a weakness — it's the whole craft.

2 · Sixty seconds of history

Early consoles showed few colors, so 8-bit art looks flat and bold. The 16-bit era loosened the limits, letting artists add shading, highlights, and depth.

3 · Your toolkit

Paid
Aseprite

The professional standard. Animation timeline, tilemaps, scripting. Your main tool.

Free
Pixelorama

Open-source all-rounder. Layers, onion-skin, tilemaps. Best free fallback.
