Form & Volume

A drawing is flat. A form looks like you could pick it up. This is how you turn shapes into volume.

Press → or Space to advance · ← to go back
What you'll take away

By the end of this lesson

  • See every object as a combo of four basic solids.
  • Understand that value describes how a surface turns to the light.
  • Tell a flat plane (hard step) from a curved surface (smooth ramp).
  • Render a flat shape into believable 3D form.
1 · The building blocks

Four solids build everything

Ateliers have taught this for centuries: almost anything is built from a sphere, cube, cylinder, and cone. A head is a sphere and a box; a tree is a sphere on a cylinder.

Master studyTake any object and mentally break it into primitives. Where are the spheres, boxes, cylinders? Trained artists do this automatically.
2 · How a surface turns

Value follows the turn

The more a surface faces the light, the lighter it is. So the way a surface turns decides how value behaves.

Name the partshighlight · core shadow (darkest, where it turns away) · reflected light (subtle rim) · cast shadow (on the ground).

5 · Guided practice

Your turn — in Aseprite

Rep 1

✓ Each reads as 3D.
Rep 2

✓ You can point to the underlying solids.
Graded assignment

Make an everyday object solid

Choose an everyday object (mug, barrel, apple, die). Note its primitive solids, then render it on a 24–32px canvas with clear 3D form from one light source, 3–5 values plus a cast shadow. Export ×8 to submissions.

We grade for
  • Reads as 3D — solid, not flat.
  • Consistent light — one direction governs every value.
  • Surface logic — curves ramp, planes step; highlight sits inside the form.
Recap

What to carry forward

  • Everything is built from sphere, cube, cylinder, cone.
  • Value describes the turn of a surface to the light.
  • Flat = hard steps; curved = smooth ramps.
