The first thing any artist learns — and the thing pixel art tests most ruthlessly: can your shapes be read at a glance?

What you'll take away

By the end of this lesson

  • Draw clean lines and curves — and spot the jaggies.
1 · The principle

Shape before detail

Every classical drawing program starts the same way: before a single detail, you establish the big shapes. Detail on a weak shape is decoration on a broken foundation.

Master studyPull up three characters you love and squint until detail disappears. What's left — the shape — does all the work.
2 · The silhouette test

If it reads in black, it works

Fill your drawing solid black and ask whether it still reads. Pixel art makes this test instant — try it:

Why it worksThe eye recognizes outer shape before interior detail. Nail the silhouette and the sprite is read before a single inside pixel is noticed.
3 · Line quality in pixels

Keep the steps consistent

A pixel line is a staircase. Even runs read clean; runs that jump around make jaggies. Curves change runs gradually.

Graded assignment

Design for the silhouette

Design a small prop or creature that reads in pure silhouette. Submit two images: the solid-black silhouette and a finished version with clean edges and minimal detail (16×16 or 24×24). Export ×8 to submissions.

We grade for
  • Silhouette reads — instantly recognizable in black.
  • Clean line — consistent runs, no jaggies or strays.
  • Restraint — detail supports the shape, never fights it.
Recap

What to carry forward

  • Shape before detail — block the big shape first.
  • The silhouette test: reads in black = strong design.
  • Clean lines come from consistent, gradually-changing runs.
